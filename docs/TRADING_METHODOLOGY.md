# Trading Methodology

Notes extracted from ADR's video transcripts that informed the indicator design.

## Core Philosophy

ADR's trading strategy has three pillars:
1. **Market structure** (trend direction: uptrend, consolidation, downtrend)
2. **Candlestick reading** (wick reactions, engulfing patterns, body size)
3. **Levels** (support/resistance zones from daily timeframe)

"Trading is simple. We are the ones that overcomplicate things."

## How ADR Draws Levels

### Starting Point
- Always starts on the **daily timeframe** (one candle = one day)
- Works backward from today: "I always start with today into the past"
- Identifies levels with **at least 2-3 touches**
- Colors levels green (support) or red (resistance)

### What Makes a High-Quality Level
- **Dual-role**: Acted as BOTH support and resistance in the past
  - "Those important levels are the ones that were resistance and then acted as support, or the other way around"
- **Multiple touches**: More touches = more important
- **Strong reactions**: Long wicks at the level = strong rejection
  - "Every time you have a wick it means a strong reaction"
  - "Wicks at the bottom means it doesn't want to go lower, bouncing fast"

### What Makes a Low-Quality Level
- Only one touch, no historical data
- No prior history as both S and R
- ADR calls these "weak levels" and treats them as secondary

### Level Hierarchy
- Previous week highs/lows (mapped first, framing the range)
- Daily timeframe levels (primary trading levels)
- Intraday levels (formed during the trading session, secondary)
- "The market moves level by level"

## How ADR Uses Levels for Trading

### Entry
- Enter at support (if bullish) or resistance (if bearish)
- Wait for **confirmation**: candlestick reaction showing the level is holding
- "My entry is always this support because the market is on an uptrend"

### Price Target
- Next major level in the direction of the trade
- "Your prize target is the next strong support/resistance"

### Stop Loss
- Beyond the wick of the rejection candle
- "My stop loss is those wicks"

### Risk/Reward
- Anything 2:1 or above is acceptable
- Prefers 3:1+ setups

## Market Structure Rules

### Uptrend
- Higher highs and higher lows
- Play long only: "There's no reason for me to try to short this"
- Entry: break of resistance, wait for pullback/retest, enter long

### Downtrend  
- Lower highs and lower lows
- Play short only: "We're going lower, the bigger move is with the trend"
- Entry: break of support, wait for retest/rejection, enter short

### Consolidation
- Range-bound, no clear trend
- Scalp between S and R: "Buy the dip, play the reaction"
- Trades must be fast: "I already know that if I'm taking a trade, that trade must be fast"
- Not suitable for swing trading

## Candlestick Reading

### Key Principles
- Shape matters more than color
- "I don't care if these candlesticks are red, green, blue, white. I only care what the candlestick is telling me"
- Bigger body = stronger sentiment/continuation
- Smaller body = indecision, losing steam

### Wick Importance
- Long wick at bottom at support = strong bounce, doesn't want to go lower
- Long wick at top at resistance = strong rejection, doesn't want to go higher
- "Wicks are very very important, at least for me"
- Used for both entry confirmation AND stop loss placement

### Engulfing Candles
- Bullish engulfing at support = likely reversal/bounce
- Bearish engulfing at resistance = likely rejection
- "Most of the time when you have an engulfing candle, the market is going to change direction"

### Confirmation
- Don't enter on first candle at a level
- Wait for confirmation that the level is holding
- Engulfing + previous resistance acting as support + higher highs/lows = triple confirmation

## Timeframe Usage

### ADR's approach (intraday/day trading)
- Daily for levels and structure
- 5-minute for entries and execution
- 30-minute for intermediate analysis

### User's approach (intra-week swing trading)
- Weekly for macro structure
- Daily for key levels and trend
- 1h / 30m for refined entries
- Does NOT use 15m or below for level drawing

## Key Quotes

- "The market moves level by level"
- "Our job as traders is not trying to guess what's the top and what's the bottom"
- "We want to go with the trend because it means buyers/sellers are winning this battle"
- "If it ain't broken, don't fix it -- if it keeps doing exactly the same thing, why try to go against it?"
- "Keep it simple. Market structure, candlestick reading, and levels. That's it."
- "Just go back to basics. I promise you if you keep it simple your trading is going to get so much better."
